Rent vs Buy Calculator: Which Builds More Wealth in 2026?

Published: June 2026 | Reading time: 5 minutes

Should you rent or buy? The answer isn't just about monthly payments — it's about which path builds more net worth over time. A good rent vs buy calculator considers home appreciation, rent increases, and what you'd earn investing your savings.

The Math: Buy vs Rent Over 10 Years

Let's compare two scenarios with realistic 2026 numbers:

Buying: $400,000 home with 10% down
Down payment: $40,000 | Loan: $360,000 at 6.11%, 30yr
Monthly PITI: ~$2,600 | Home appreciates 3%/year
After 10 years: Home value = $537,567 | Remaining loan = $300,000 | Equity = $237,567
Renting: $2,200/month, increase 3%/year
Invest the $40,000 down payment at 7% in stock market
Invest monthly savings (buying cost - renting cost) each month
After 10 years: Invested portfolio ≈ $175,000–$210,000

When Buying Wins

  • You plan to stay 5+ years (transaction costs eat short-term gains)
  • Home prices in your area appreciate at 3%+/year
  • Rents are rising faster than inflation
  • You want to lock in a fixed housing cost (mortgage stays flat, rent goes up)
  • You can itemize deductions and benefit from the mortgage interest tax deduction

When Renting Wins

  • You plan to move in fewer than 3 years
  • Your local price-to-rent ratio is above 20 (homes are overpriced vs. renting)
  • You don't have a 20% down payment and would pay PMI
  • You're in a high property tax state where the tax burden tilts the math
  • You want flexibility and don't want to deal with maintenance, repairs, or HOAs

The Hidden Costs of Buying Most Calculators Miss

  • Closing costs: 2–5% upfront — that's $8,000–$20,000 on a $400,000 home
  • Maintenance: Budget 1% of home value per year ($4,000 on $400,000)
  • Property taxes: Can double your effective monthly cost in high-tax states
  • Selling costs: 6% realtor commission when you sell
